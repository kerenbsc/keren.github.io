MERCURY

Statistics

  • Distance from Sun: Approximately 0.387 AU (57.91 million km)
  • Diameter: 4881.06 km ( 3032.95 miles )
  • Length of Day (one complete rotation on its axis): 176 Earth days
  • Length of Year (one complete orbit around the Sun): 88 Earth days
  • Temperature during daytime: 800°F (430°C)
  • Temperature during nighttime: -290°F (-180°C)
  • Atmosphere: Composed of helium, hydrogen, oxygen, sodium, calcium, potassium, and water vapor
  • Moons: 0

Overview

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. Its surface is covered in craters, much like Earth’s Moon, giving it a rugged and ancient appearance. Despite being so close to the Sun, Mercury doesn’t have a thick atmosphere to trap heat, which leads to extreme temperature changes between day and night.

Facts

A single day on Mercury lasts longer than its year. While it takes just 88 Earth days to orbit the Sun, it rotates very slowly on its axis. This unusual motion, combined with its lack of atmosphere, creates one of the most extreme environments in the solar system.
