VENUS

Statistics

  • Distance from Sun: Approximately 0.723 AU ( 109 million km )
  • Diameter: 12,104 km ( 7,521 miles ) at the equator
  • Length of Day (one complete rotation on its axis): 243 Earth days
  • Length of Year (one complete orbit around the Sun): 224.7 Earth days
  • Temperature throughout the day : 867°F (462°C)
  • Atmosphere: Composed of carbon dioxide and sulfuric acid with a thick cloud layer
  • Moons: 0

Overview

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and the closest planetary neighbor to Earth. It's the third brightest object in the sky after the Sun and Moon, and it rotates very slowly in the opposite direction to most other planets.

Often called Earth's “twin” because of its similar size and structure, Venus is very different in conditions. Its thick atmosphere traps heat in a powerful greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet in our solar system, with surface temperatures high enough to melt lead—around 467°C (872°F). The pressure at its surface is also extreme, about 93 times stronger than Earth's at sea level. Beneath its dense clouds, Venus has a rocky landscape shaped by volcanoes and warped mountain ranges.

Facts

Spending a day on Venus would feel incredibly disorienting; assuming you could even survive its intense heat and crushing atmospheric pressure. A single day there lasts about 243 Earth days, which is actually longer than a full Venusian year (225 Earth days). Because the planet rotates so slowly, it takes around 117 Earth days just to go from sunrise to sunset. Even more unusual, the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east due to Venus rotating in the opposite direction to Earth. You also wouldn't get any break from the extreme temperatures. On Earth, our 23‐degree axial tilt creates seasons by changing how directly sunlight reaches different regions. Venus, however, is tilted by only about three degrees;far too small to create noticeable seasonal changes. Hence, it's harsh conditions remain constant year-round.
